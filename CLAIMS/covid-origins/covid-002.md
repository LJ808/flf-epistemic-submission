### E-Prime Rewrite

### Analysis
"This means" smuggles a deduction as automatic, hiding its conditional structure. E-Prime resists "means" without restructuring, forcing the *if...then...contingent on* chain into view. This makes visible exactly where a counter-rebuttal needs to attack: the single source's reliability.

That is, in fact, precisely where Peter attacked in his direct response (documented in the same transcript): Chen's hospitalization records reportedly show December 16 onset — after, not before, the first wet-market case — directly contradicting the Daily Mail's December 8 claim. The standard summary's "claims of earlier cases are unreliable" compresses this entire chain into one adjective, hiding which specific link (source reliability) the unreliability attaches to, and therefore obscuring exactly how the rebuttal works.
